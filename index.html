Dr. Surendra Pratap Singh is a distinguished Plant Pathologist and Scientific Officer with over 28 years of dedicated research in sugarcane disease science at premier government institutions across Uttar Pradesh.

Holding a Ph.D. in Plant Pathology from DDU Gorakhpur University, he has built an outstanding legacy of contributions to varietal resistance screening, molecular diagnostics, and fungicide efficacy — work that has directly shaped disease management strategies across India's sugarcane belt.

With 95 publications spanning peer-reviewed journals, Springer book chapters, and popular extension literature — plus participation in national and international conferences — Dr. Singh is a recognized authority whose insights bridge laboratory science and field application.

Key AICRP Sugarcane Projects
PP14 · Red Rot Pathotype Identification
Identified & maintained virulent isolates; led to declaration of new Indian sub-tropical pathotype CF13 originating from Co 0238 variety.
PP17 · Pre-zonal Variety Resistance
205 entries tested for red rot, smut and yellow leaf; 150 found resistant/moderately resistant.
PP23 · Elite Genotype Screening
55 ISH/IGH genotypes evaluated; 34 found R/MR against red rot.
PP31 · Pokkahboeng Epidemiology
Genotype evaluation with disease incidence correlated to climatic conditions; varietal susceptibility profiling.
PP33 · Yellow Leaf Disease
Meristem culture + molecular diagnostics combined; 2280 hardened seedlings produced and verified disease-free.
PP34 · Mechanized Fungicide Delivery
Sett treatment with soil drenching at 45th & 90th day proven most effective against red rot primary infection in field conditions.
